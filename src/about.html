ABOUT

When you go out to Manezhnaya Square, attention is attracted by a massive palace-type building with Corinthian order columns and Venetian windows above them. Large cinema advertising posters and showcases, and in the evenings neon lights greet the audience at the Cinema House.

And above all these signs of today stands the original cartouche of the building with the image of three balls - a medieval symbol of usury. Cartouche reminds that the palace of profit of enterprising St. Petersburg merchants was built here. However, history ordered otherwise. No sooner had the Petrograd Provincial Credit Society settled in its new building than it lost it in the days of October.

This building on Karavannaya Street (B. Tolmacheva), which closed the ensemble of Manezhnaya Square, was built in 1914-1916 according to the project of architects - graduates of the Academy of Arts K.S. Bobrovsky and B.Ya. Botkin. The authors designed the house in the character of an Italian palazzo of late Palladian architecture of the XVI century.

In addition to the bank, for commercial reasons, a concert auditorium and shops were built in the basement of the building. The first cinema opened here in 1917 and became one of the best in Petrograd.

On November 7, 1918, the premiere of the first Soviet feature film based on Lunacharsky's script "The Seal", directed by A. Panteleev, G. Pashkovskaya and A. Dolinov, took place here. Interestingly, in 1924, a young D.D. Shostakovich, who was then a student at the conservatory, worked here as a piano player in the evenings.

After the Great Patriotic War, in 1947-48, a large repair was carried out. On June 12, 1948, the first children's cinema in Leningrad opened with the new name "Rodina".

A new period of life of this building began in 1959. Major reconstruction of the upper floors was carried out, the auditorium was restored. The cinematographic center of our city, the Union of Cinematographers of St. Petersburg, has been operating here since 1960 and the well-known cinema hall of the Cinema House is located here. Today, the Cinema House offers the audience masterpieces of both Russian and foreign cinema, introduces the best examples of modern author's cinema. The cinema center hosts retrospective screenings, film festivals, and creative evenings. During the year, leading film critics and film critics of the city read exclusive thematic cycles of lectures on cinematography. Exhibitions of painting and photography are constantly held in the lobby of the Cinema House.
